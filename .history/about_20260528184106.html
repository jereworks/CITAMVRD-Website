Our Heritage

The Journey of Divine Expansion

From a tiny localized expatriate prayer group into a cross-border kingdom assignment spanning tens of thousands of souls.

1952

The Visionary Seed

PAOC Missionary John McBride arrives in Nairobi with an apostolic mandate. Initial English-speaking multi-racial worship clusters convene in Rahimtulla Hall near Jevanjee Street.

1960

The Valley Road Foundation

The ministry structurally relocates to 28 Valley Road. Rev. Richard Bombay completes construction of the very first sanctuary, dedicated on October 22, 1960, under the name Pentecostal Evangelistic Centre (PEC).

1970s

Transition to NPC

Under the strategic pastorate of Rev. Mervyn Thomas, the church expands and updates its nomenclature to Nairobi Pentecostal Church (NPC). The structural cross-shield logo and initial institutional constitution are drafted.

1980s

African Leadership & Explosive Growth

Following historical accelerations under Rev. Roy Upton and Rev. Dennis White, leadership smoothly shifts to Bishop Bonifes Adoyo, NPC’s first indigenous African Presiding Leader, marking monumental local scaling.

2003

Birth of CITAM Autonomous System

Nairobi Pentecostal Church rebrands officially to Christ Is the Answer Ministries (CITAM), registering as a completely autonomous independent entity under the Societies Act of Kenya to execute global operations.

2020 - Present

Strategic Future Deployment

The ministry governance baton passes down to the current Presiding Bishop, Rev. Calisto Odede. CITAM scales to over 38 unified assemblies locally and internationally, governing media platforms (HOPE FM/TV), schools, and university complexes.
